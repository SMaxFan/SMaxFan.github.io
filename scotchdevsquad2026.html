Week 0 Homework

It was nice to read your work this week, and to get to know you over Zoom. If you haven't scheduled a time to speak, please make sure you do that via email.

Things to work on:

  • From now on, arguments should come in the "Framing, Mechanism, Outcome, Impact" form. If you are uncertain about what this means, you should go through some of the sample work. I spoke and made notes about it in almost every case. This should cut down the time you are spending writing these cases since irrelevant material will fall away.
  • Be wary of principles. They are often practically contingent. Even in the case of a very strong principle (e.g. against killing) there are some exceptions (e.g. someone experiencing terminal and excruciating pain on the battle field, euthanasia). How we determine these is, more often than not, based on whether it is good for the invdiviudal, which is a practical determination. I suggest you avoid these for now.
  • Try to read in a targeted way. Next week, I'll try to distribute a list of reputable news sources and books to read to kick off your research. Remember that, in part, the research is a way of getting you to read about the world around you and to teach you to read faster. I expect it to be slow-going for the first few weeks and to speed up later. If you don't do the first part, you won't become fast at it.
